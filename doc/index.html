Testing GPIO pins on Raspberry Pi 2

The official GPIO usage documentation seems a bit thin to me, but it is official.

The GPIO pin numbering scheme used in this document starts in the upper left of the GPIO block and proceeds in "English reading order" — left-to-right, top-to-bottom. In other words the top row are pins 1 and 2, the next row are pins 3 and 4, and so on, until the final row consisting of pins 39 and 40. See the pin-out diagram and other details at http://elinux.org/RPi_Low-level_peripherals#Model_A.2B.2C_B.2B_and_B2. Also this Interactive Pinout diagram at http://pinout.xyz/ provided a lot of info. Click the pin you are interested in to find out more about it.

The recommendation is, once you gain facility with the beastie, switch to the GPIO (a.k.a. Broadcom or BCM) pin numbering system, even though it doesn't follow any particularly logical ordering.

  • The red wire is the 3.3 volt power supply, connected to Pin 1 on the Raspberry Pi 2
  • The black wire is the ground wire and connects to Pin 9.
  • The resistor is a 1KΩ (brown, black, red) resistor.
  • The blue wire is the OUTPUT tester.
  • The yellow wire is the INPUT tester.

So.

First, connect the blue wire to pin 39 (GND) and see if the LED lights up. If it doesn't, flip it 180° so that the wires are the other way around, and try again. If it still doesn't work, then you may have a bad LED or a shorted wire. Check to be sure you have things wired as described above and replace wires and LEDs until you have a successful lighting.
